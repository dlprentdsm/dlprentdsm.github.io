P.O.W.E.R.

Jan 14, 2014 / Random Musings /

For one of my classes recently I had to summarize something called the POWER program (see here). The POWER program is the Five Simple Steps to Achieving Success in Business and in Life. This is my summary for my class. Put in on your refrigerator for some daily motivation... Read the Post

Prepare:

Proper preparation is key to any activity- you can't swim after you've eaten and you can't go to sleep if you've just had a gallon of coffee. In any aspect of your life you can't get the desired result without taking the time for the necessary preparation. A meeting as an activity just like any other activity, and like any other activity it requires the proper preparation. Without this preparation you waste your time, your client's time, and also look like a buffoon.

Open:

As Dale Carnegie would probably say, first impressions matter. Immediately establishing dominance through gaze, posture and positioning is key to success as a CEO but such behavior runs counter to our desire as a consultant to develop a partnership with the client. Therefore we should seek to develop rapport through a friendly gaze and a welcoming office space, along with cultivating a professional appearance that engenders trust. But its not just impressions that matter, but substance. Therefore the "Open" also consists of laying down an outline of the meeting so that both parties know exactly what to expect, and if these goals are even feasible.

Work:

This is the middle of the sandwich, and it is where we actually accomplish what we want to accomplish. But just as a sandwich without bread is not actually a sandwich, we cannot accomplish our goals without laying the groundwork for success by preparing for the meeting and successfully opening the meeting. Failure at either of those points undermines our ability to accomplish the Work we want to accomplish at this point.

End:

Every story needs an ending, but the End is more than this. It is not only an ending but a beginning. It is the beginning of our next meeting, and a successful End establishes exactly what we hope to accomplish before the next meeting. When we Prepare for the next meeting we will be meeting objectives that we established in the End. Since preparation is the key to success, it follows through some sort of logical transitivity that a successful End is also the key to success.

Reflect:

Don't skip this step. Are you like 99% percent of the losers out there who are content to remain where they are, never rising, simply floating idly, flotsam adrift on a sea of mediocrity? No! You are a go-getter! You are the 1% with the burning ambition to succeed, to rise above humble origins and make something of yourself. Reflection is what will separate you from the herd. The 99% just do a job and leave it at that. The 99% skip the last step. They go home to dull, second-rate lives and sit in front of the telly watching the soaps and reading tabloids. But you don't want a second-rate life. You are different. You want a first-rate life, and to earn that you realize that you'll have to set yourself apart, and be better than the rest. How will you do that? Through Reflection. When you finish a job, you'll reflect on it and think about how you can improve. Constant reflection and self-improvement is what will make you the 1%.
