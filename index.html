About Me.

I am a fourth year PhD student in Statistics at the University of Minnesota. Data is my passion.

Who I Am

My name is Daniel Prentice and I am pursuing my PhD in Statistics at the University of Minnesota. I want to tap big data to optimize the world.

I was born and raised in Portland, OR, where I ran a successful non-profit before leaving for Grinnell College, where I received a BA in Mathematics with Honors in 2011. After a year spent wandering the Middle East I came to Minneapolis to pursure graduate studies in Statistics and prepare myself for the data revolution. My current research focus is global optimization for Difference of Convex (DC) functions, which form the basis for many state of the art statistical models and real world optimization problems. More broadly I'm interesting in optimization, large p data, and generally all aspects of data science.

Consulting

I consult about research design, statistical methodology and data analysis. Currently I work as a Data Scientist at a technology company where I am tasked with constantly creating new value from data. In the past I have consulted on research into Swine Flu genotypes and transmission with faculty in the Department of Animal Science and Veterinary Medicine, and worked as a summer researcher at Microsoft Research Asia where I helped develop a specific type of semantic search capability (matching instances of two classes by relevancy, given a predicate) for the Bing search engine. I was one of four founding consultants at the Data Analysis and Social Inquiry Lab at Grinnell College, which specialized in providing support to interdisciplinary research activities of the faculty. You can see some of my past projects here. Please contact me to set up a consultation.

Tutoring

I have been tutoring in Mathematics and Statistics for the past 8 years, and have a passion for helping others learn. I've tutored since my time at Grinnell College and I now offer my services to both University of Minnesota students and the wider community. In addition to statistics, I can help with any math, physics, psychology or econ course at the middle school, high school, or college level. I can also help with computing, including R, C and basic programming concepts. Please see my tutoring page here.
